Abstract

        As urbanization accelerates, cities worldwide face significant challenges related to pollution and sustainability. Singapore, recognized as a leading smart city, has implemented a range of innovative strategies to manage these pressing issues. With a population of 6.04 million residing within 750 square kilometres, Singapore has prioritized the use of technology and data-driven solutions to enhance urban living while mitigating environmental impacts. This article explores Singapore's comprehensive approach to reducing air pollution, including the strategic relocation of polluting industries, stringent emission standards, and substantial investments in research for sustainable technologies. By examining these initiatives, the article highlights how Singapore successfully balances economic growth with environmental responsibility, serving as a model for other urban centres striving for a cleaner, healthier future.
